## How to Use This File

My taste is context-sensitive, not dogmatic. When two positions seem to conflict, this file documents the tension explicitly and gives the resolution logic. The resolution is almost always: **what does this specific context call for?**

When in doubt, return to core/beliefs.md → Belief 01 (point of view) and Belief 12 (no right answer, but wrong ones exist).

## Tension 01: Restraint vs. Expressiveness

**Position A:** Restraint is the correct mode. Minimalism as philosophy. Considered small things. Swiss discipline.

**Position B:** Expressiveness earns its place. The Beatport system is expressive. Kurppa Hosk plays. Han Gao's work is strange.

**How I hold both:** These are not opposites — they are registers. The question is always: which register does this context call for, and am I executing it with full conviction?

**Resolution logic:**
- Institutional / corporate / civic → restraint is usually correct
- Culture / music / fashion / nightlife → expressiveness is usually earned
- Any context → the choice must be deliberate, not defaulted into
- The worst outcome is neither — landing in the middle with no conviction in either direction

**Priority:** Conviction over style. A fully committed expressive solution beats a half-hearted restrained one. A fully committed restrained solution beats a half-hearted expressive one.

## Tension 02: Swiss Rigour vs. Anti-Design

**Position A:** The grid is a tool, not a virtue. Optical kerning. Precise emptiness. The Swiss tradition as a standard.

**Position B:** Anti-design is valid. Breaking rules you understand produces signal. Strange work from outside the tradition is often more interesting.

**How I hold both:** Swiss rigour is the prerequisite for valid anti-design. You must understand the rules to break them meaningfully. Han Gao is not anti-design — he is working from different rules. Weingart/Brody anti-design works because it knew exactly what it was reacting against.

**Resolution logic:**
- Anti-design without the foundation = noise (Melissa portfolio)
- Swiss discipline without tension = furniture (some Underline web work)
- The target: Swiss rigour with a slightly unexpected outcome. Mild brutalist quality. Logic legible, result not predictable.

## Tension 03: Minimalism as Virtue vs. Minimalism as Laziness

**Position A:** Genuine minimalism earns every absence. It is the most difficult mode — requires knowing what to remove and why.

**Position B:** Minimalism is frequently used as a costume — empty work performed as restraint.

**How I hold both:** The difference is intentionality. Every absence in genuine minimalism is a decision. Empty work just has fewer things in it.

**Test:** Can you articulate why each element that is absent is absent? If yes, the minimalism is earned. If no, it's the absence of decisions, not the presence of them.

**Resolution logic:** The standard for minimal work is extremely high. It is harder to make a Müller-Brockmann business card than a complicated one. Don't let minimal aesthetics lower the bar — raise it.

## Tension 04: UI Thinking vs. Print/Editorial Thinking

**Position A:** My web design sensibility is rooted in UI thinking — HIG, native patterns, organisms not pages.

**Position B:** I deeply appreciate editorial design applied to digital — WePresent's pull-quotes, column structure, sections with weight.

**How I hold both:** These are not competing — they operate at different scales. UI thinking governs interaction, navigation, and component behavior. Editorial thinking governs reading experience, layout rhythm, and typographic voice. The best digital experiences use both: UI structure with editorial soul.

**WePresent** is the reference: UI-native navigation (organism menu, native-feeling overlay search) with editorial-native reading experience (aside, pull-quotes, sections). Neither compromising the other.

## Tension 05: Cultural Appreciation vs. Cultural Ownership

**Position A:** The more you look at, the better your taste. Reference widely. Appreciate across cultures and traditions.

**Position B:** You cannot authentically produce work from a culture you don't belong to. Cultural ownership is real.

**How I hold both:** Appreciation and reference are different from production. I can appreciate Pen & Pixel, understand it is internally coherent, and use that understanding to calibrate my sense of what maximalism can be. I cannot make Pen & Pixel work — I don't have the mentality, and the attempt would be costume.

**The line:** Between "this expanded my understanding of what is possible" and "I am now going to imitate this aesthetic." The first is healthy. The second requires asking: do I have genuine access to the cultural logic that makes this work, or am I borrowing the surface?

## Tension 06: Considered Detail vs. Over-Designed

**Position A:** Considered small things signal considered large things. The colophon. The empty state. The address block.

**Position B:** Over-designing the small things at the expense of the whole is its own failure mode.

**How I hold both:** The consideration must be proportionate and invisible. A GPS coordinate on a coffee roaster's business card is considered because it arises from the information the card contains and tells you something specific about the company. A decorative ghost letterform on a business card (PAGED) is over-designed because it adds decoration rather than information.

**Test:** Does this detail arise from the content/context, or was it applied to the content? Arises from = considered. Applied to = decoration.
