## Branding / Identity

**Beatport rebrand (Kurppa Hosk)**
→ Considered small things. Address orientation as compositional decision. UI and print working from same typographic logic. System that can be expressive and restrained simultaneously.

**Unlocked system (Kurppa Hosk)**
→ Concept made physical. Word fragmented across grid — you work to read it, the effort is the concept. Doesn't shy from showing the grid's technicalness.

**Fatal logotype**
→ Point of view. Irreducible. Could not put another name in this treatment and have it remain the same object. Has a stance.

**E4 logo**
→ Crosshair/plus constructed from letterforms, greyscale fill differentiating characters. Sometimes you cannot explain why something is good. The limit of verbal explanation of visual logic. Sometimes correct is felt before it is named.

**Perfumehead "La La Love" campaign**
→ Type as image. Large bold type filling the frame, product photograph centered, restrained. The type is the campaign. Word/image where both do equal work.

**Omini System credentials (Han Gao)**
→ Bilingual Chinese/Latin interleaved. Kappa logo as graphic element. Colour blocks as only differentiator. No over-branding. Typography trusted to carry everything.

## Web / Digital

**WePresent (Molly)**
→ Editorial done correctly for digital. Nav as organism (three-column expansion). Pull-quotes animate on scroll. Sections have weight. Footer narrower than body. Empty search state designed. Blog done right.

**Robinhood Market product page**
→ E-commerce as UI system. Thumbnail carousel right. Product as keynote slide. CTA persistent left. Size picker as popover organism. Details as clipping-mask accordion. Every interactable element immediately legible.

**Molly Studio site**
→ Modal/carousel architecture. No traditional pages. Text ~400-500px max. Native-feeling. Antithesis of tech startup website.

**shopify.design**
→ App not document. WebGL mode via click-and-hold pill. DOM and WebGL synced to native scroll. Experience first.

**rauno.me**
→ Plain with maximum care. Figma-frame horizontal nav. Minimap. Parallax oversized text. Every unusual decision solves a real problem.

**The Ordinary website**
→ Performs minimalism. Name is self-referential irony that collapses into generic. Non-Name does the concept correctly — commits without winking.

## Print / Editorial

**Stone Lullaby #2 tracklist**
→ Proportions as design. Text in corner of enormous white field. No image. Whitespace as content.

**Tokyo Metropolitan Teien Art Museum invitation (Han Gao)**
→ Institution name as interlocking typographic mosaic. Type is the image. Information is the design.

**Josef Müller-Brockmann business card**
→ Exact emptiness. Not generous minimalism — precise. Reference for total restraint as total commitment.

**Four Hundred Coffee card**
→ Mono type, field labels, GPS coordinates. Unexpected detail arising from the information itself, not applied from outside.

**Park Books back cover**
→ Margin labels + indented hierarchy. Decoration: none. Hierarchy from position and weight alone.

**Autechre Envane EP (Designers Republic)**
→ Visual language with its own physics. Both too-cool conditions met. Can't think it, can't execute it. Alien cultural coordinates made rigorous.

## Works I Dislike — What They Fail

**Park Road condo bag**
→ Generic by refinement. Optima serif on beautiful bag. Could say anything. The object has more design intelligence than the brand on it.

**Bronx River Greenway**
→ First idea. River shape + green + B. No lateral movement. Performs community representation while being a poster child of playing it safe.

**Swatch "Your Time, Your Rules"**
→ Watch invisible in watch ad. Type illegible against photography. Incoherent register (luxury + accessible youth culture, fluent in neither). Product removable without the campaign changing.

**Feararchy "Ego Death"**
→ Metahaven without the thinking. Borrows visual language gravity without doing the intellectual work.

**Junoon (student book)**
→ Strong concept, weak editing. Everything at maximum simultaneously. Two outlined fonts with different strokes. Double-spaced body (reads as Word). Paragraph spacing wider than heading-to-body (hierarchy failure).

**Moon Media portfolio**
→ Not a designer. No spatial, typographic, or aesthetic logic present. Canva listed next to Figma as equivalent tools. Devalues the profession.

**Melissa portfolio**
→ Stretched display type alongside Helvetica body copy. Anti-design gesture in headline, conventional body. Neither choice supported by the other. Declaration without commitment.
