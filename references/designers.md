## Individual Designer Positions
*Last updated: 2026-05-10*

## Rauno Fischbacher (previously Vercel, Shopify orbit)
**Practice:** Developer/designer at the intersection of engineering and interaction design. rauno.me
**Exemplifies:** Plain done with maximum care. Interaction design grounded in physics and metaphor. The correct personal site.
**Key writing:** "Invisible Details of Interaction Design," "Designing Depth," "Novelty," "Contrasting Aesthetics" — all directly load-bearing on how I think about motion and interaction.
**What his site demonstrates:** Figma-frame metaphor for portfolio navigation. Minimap. Parallax of oversized text for legibility of cropped titles. Earns every unusual interaction decision by solving a real problem.
**Position:** One of the clearest thinkers on interaction design writing publicly. His vocabulary for why things feel right is better than most published UI writing.

## Han Gao (Shanghai)
**Practice:** Graphic designer, text-based experimental practice. Tokyo TDC, Graphic Design Festival Scotland.
**Exemplifies:** Design as intellectual practice rather than problem-solving. Text as primary material with spatial, semantic, and perceptual properties. Chinese design scene operating from different assumptions than Western tradition.
**Key position:** "Beauty doesn't come first. The concept and idea does." Not used as cover for aesthetic decisions — his work looks like it does *because* of how it thinks.
**Notable work:** Bilingual typographic compositions treating Chinese and Latin scripts as structurally equivalent. "A Conjecture" (reading field experiments). "On the Move" poster.
**Too cool test:** Can't think the idea, partially can't execute it. The mentality is different, not just the craft.
**What to steal:** The willingness to treat text as research material, not just as information carrier.

## Josef Müller-Brockmann
**Practice:** Swiss modernist, foundational grid theorist.
**Exemplifies:** Exact emptiness. The business card is the reference object: name top right, studio/private address bottom in two parallel columns. Not generous minimalism — precise.
**Position:** The origin point of the Swiss tradition I work within and against. Understanding his work is prerequisite to understanding what you are doing when you use a grid.

## Paul Rand
**Practice:** American modernist, corporate identity.
**Exemplifies:** IBM, ABC, UPS — identity as reduction to essential mark.
**Position:** Business card: "Paul Rand / Paul Rand Inc / Goodhill Road Weston Connecticut 06883 / Tel: 203/227". Nothing else. The most confident business card possible. Use as reference for when restraint is total commitment, not just aesthetic.

## Damien Correll (Figma, Creative Director)
**Practice:** Presentation design, design culture.
**Exemplifies:** Deck as collaboration artifact. Idea first, structure second.
**Key writing:** "Steal This Deck" — structure, format, common mistakes.
**Personal position:** Presentations should be idea-first, not structure-first. The idea describes the structure, not the other way around. See meta/presentations.md.

---

## Carl Rivera (Shopify Design)
**Practice:** Design leadership, team pace, product design culture.
**Key writing:** "Deciding to Move Faster" — indecision masquerades as progress; speed compounds; daily units of measurement.
**Position:** The quality that matters above all is pace. Teams that move fast build fast, learn fast, get better faster. This is not about rushing — it's about getting to decisions sooner. Indecision is the enemy.
**Application:** How I think about studio process and creative brief workflow.

---

## Wolfgang Weingart / Ed Fella / Neville Brody
**Practice:** Foundational anti-design / new wave typography.
**Exemplifies:** The valid tradition that anti-design comes from — questioning the neutrality of Swiss modernism, asserting the designer's subjectivity. Punk, post-punk, early personal computer typographic experimentation.
**Position:** The intellectual history that makes anti-design a legitimate position when practiced from understanding. The tradition students skip when they arrive at anti-design aesthetics without the foundation.
**Note:** Flag for deeper research — these are referenced but not yet analyzed in depth.
